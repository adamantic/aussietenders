**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 2026
**Status:** MVP Planning

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Vision
GovTender Pro is an AI-powered platform that democratizes access to government contracts for Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs). By simplifying tender discovery, automating application preparation, and providing competitive intelligence, we enable SMEs to compete effectively in the $50B+ Australian government procurement market.

### 1.2 Problem Statement
SMEs face significant barriers when pursuing government contracts:
- **Discovery complexity**: Tenders are scattered across multiple portals (NSW eTendering, AusTender) with inconsistent formats
- **Time-intensive research**: Understanding tender requirements requires significant manual effort
- **Application burden**: Repetitive data entry across applications (company details, capabilities, past performance)
- **Competitive blindness**: No visibility into who else is bidding or historical win patterns
- **Resource constraints**: Cannot afford dedicated tender writers or business development teams

### 1.3 Solution
A unified platform that:
1. Aggregates tenders from NSW and Federal sources into a single, searchable interface
2. Uses AI to summarize and analyze tender requirements
3. Auto-fills applications with ABN/ASIC company data
4. Provides competitive intelligence and win rate analytics
5. Connects users with tender writing professionals when needed

### 1.4 Key Differentiators
| Feature | AusTender | NSW eTendering | TenderSearch | **GovTender Pro** |
|---------|-----------|----------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Unified search | Federal only | NSW only | Aggregator | NSW + Federal |
| AI summaries | No | No | No | **Yes** |
| Auto-fill from ABN | No | No | No | **Yes** |
| Competitor analytics | No | No | Limited | **Comprehensive** |
| Writer marketplace | No | No | No | **Yes** |

## 2. Target Users

### 2.1 Primary Personas

#### Persona 1: SME Business Owner
- **Profile**: Owner/operator of 5-50 person company
- **Industries**: Construction, IT services, professional services, facilities management
- **Pain points**:
  - Spends 10+ hours/week searching for relevant tenders
  - Misses opportunities due to deadline management failures
  - Lacks resources for dedicated BD staff
- **Goals**:
  - Find relevant opportunities quickly
  - Reduce time spent on repetitive application tasks
  - Improve win rates through better targeting

#### Persona 2: Tender Writer / Consultant
- **Profile**: Freelance or agency-based tender specialist
- **Pain points**:
  - Finding clients who need their services
  - Tracking multiple clients' tender pipelines
  - Demonstrating value through win rate data
- **Goals**:
  - Access new client leads
  - Streamline research process
  - Build reputation through platform reviews

#### Persona 3: Business Development Manager
- **Profile**: BD professional at medium-sized company (50-200 employees)
- **Pain points**:
  - Managing large tender pipeline
  - Making informed bid/no-bid decisions
  - Reporting on BD metrics to leadership
- **Goals**:
  - Comprehensive market visibility
  - Data-driven decision making
  - Clear win/loss analytics

Discovery → Evaluation → Preparation → Submission → Outcome Tracking
    ↓           ↓            ↓             ↓              ↓

## 3. Core Features

### 3.1 Tender Discovery & Search

#### 3.1.1 Unified Tender Feed
- **Sources**:
  - NSW eTendering API (state contracts)
  - AusTender (federal contracts)
- **Data sync**: Real-time or near-real-time updates (polling interval TBD based on API limits)
- **Tender data captured**:
  - Title, description, agency
  - Category/UNSPSC codes
  - Location requirements
  - Contract value (if disclosed)
  - Open/close dates
  - Document attachments (links)

#### 3.1.2 Search & Filtering
- **Keyword search**: Full-text search across title, description, requirements
- **Filters**:
  - Category/industry (UNSPSC codes)
  - Location (state, region, national)
  - Contract value range
  - Closing date range
  - Status (open, closed, awarded)
  - Source (NSW, Federal)
- **Saved searches**: Users can save filter combinations for quick access

#### 3.1.3 AI-Powered Summaries
- **Technology**: Claude API (Anthropic)
- **Summary content**:
  - Plain-English description of what's being procured
  - Key requirements and mandatory criteria
  - Evaluation criteria breakdown
  - Estimated effort/complexity indicator
  - Key dates and milestones
- **Storage**: Summaries stored in database for:
  - Fast retrieval (no re-processing)
  - Future ML training for matching improvements
  - Semantic search capabilities (future)

### 3.2 Smart Matching & Alerts

#### 3.2.1 Matching Algorithm (MVP)
```
Match Score = weighted sum of:
  - Industry code match (40%)
  - Location match (20%)
  - Contract value fit (20%)
  - Keyword relevance (20%)
```

#### 3.2.2 Email Notifications
- **New matches**: Daily digest of new tenders matching saved preferences
- **Deadline reminders**:
  - 7 days before close
  - 3 days before close
  - 1 day before close
- **Award notifications**: When tracked tenders are awarded
- **User preferences**: Configurable frequency (daily/weekly), categories, value thresholds

### 3.3 Application Assistance

#### 3.3.1 Company Profile Auto-Fill (MVP - Free)
- **Data sources**:
  - ABN Lookup API: ABN, business name, GST status, location
  - ASIC API: Directors, shareholders, company type, registration date
- **Auto-fill fields**:
  - Legal entity name
  - ABN/ACN
  - Registered address
  - Director names
  - Years in operation
  - GST registration status

#### 3.3.2 Document Generation (Future - Paid)
- **Capability statements**: Template-based generation from profile data
- **Common response templates**: Pre-written answers to standard tender questions
- **Custom document builder**: Drag-and-drop section assembly

### 3.4 Pipeline Management

#### 3.4.1 Kanban Board
- **Stages**:
  1. **Discovered**: New matches, not yet reviewed
  2. **Evaluating**: Under consideration, reviewing requirements
  3. **Preparing**: Decision to bid, working on application
  4. **Submitted**: Application lodged, awaiting outcome
  5. **Won**: Contract awarded to user
  6. **Lost**: Contract awarded to competitor
  7. **No Bid**: Decided not to pursue

#### 3.4.2 Tender Cards
- Display: Title, agency, value, close date, status, match score
- Quick actions: Move stage, add notes, view details

### 3.5 Competitive Intelligence

#### 3.5.1 Win History (from public award notices)
- **Data source**: Scrape/API award notices from government portals
- **Display**:
  - Winning company name
  - Contract value
  - Date awarded
- **Search**: Find historical wins by company name, category, or tender keywords

#### 3.5.2 Competitor Tracking
- **Watchlist**: Save competitor company names
- **Alerts**: Notify when watched competitors win contracts
- **Company profiles**: Aggregate view of competitor's win history

#### 3.5.3 Market Share Analytics
- **Category breakdown**: Which companies dominate each category
- **Win rate benchmarks**: Anonymous aggregate win rates by category/value
- **Trend analysis**: Market share shifts over time

### 3.6 Analytics Dashboard

#### 3.6.1 Personal Performance
- Win rate (overall and by category)
- Total contract value won
- Pipeline value (sum of opportunities in active stages)
- Application-to-win conversion funnel

#### 3.6.2 Benchmarking (Future - Paid)
- Compare personal win rate to platform averages
- Industry-specific benchmarks
- Recommendations based on historical patterns

### 3.7 Tender Writer Marketplace

#### 3.7.1 Writer Directory
- **Listing fields**:
  - Name, company, bio
  - Industry specializations
  - Geographic coverage
  - Price range (indicative)
  - Verified credentials
  - Platform reviews/ratings
- **Search/filter**: By industry, location, price, rating

#### 3.7.2 Connection Flow
1. User browses directory
2. User views writer profile
3. User sends inquiry (message + tender details)
4. Writer responds with availability/quote
5. Parties connect off-platform (email/phone)

#### 3.7.3 Writer Onboarding
- Application form with credentials
- Manual verification (initially)
- Profile creation
- Subscription or commission model (TBD)

## 4. Technical Architecture

### 4.1 Platform Stack

|-------|------------|-----------|
| Hosting | **Replit** | Rapid deployment, easy scaling, built-in DB |

### 4.3 External API Integrations

#### 4.3.1 NSW eTendering API
- **Base URL**: `https://tenders.nsw.gov.au/`
- **Authentication**: API key (apply via portal)
- **Endpoints needed**:
  - Search/list tenders
  - Tender details
  - Award notices
- **Rate limits**: TBD (check documentation)
- **Sync strategy**: Scheduled job every 15-30 minutes

#### 4.3.2 AusTender API
- **Base URL**: `https://www.tenders.gov.au/`
- **Authentication**: Open data (no auth required for public data)
- **Data format**: XML/JSON feeds
- **Endpoints needed**:
  - ATM (Approach to Market) listings
  - Contract notices
  - Standing offers
- **Sync strategy**: Scheduled job every 15-30 minutes

#### 4.3.3 ABN Lookup API
- **Provider**: Australian Business Register
- **Base URL**: `https://abr.business.gov.au/`
- **Authentication**: GUID-based API key (free registration)
- **Data returned**:
  - Business name (current and historical)
  - ABN status
  - Entity type
  - GST registration
  - Main business location

#### 4.3.4 ASIC API
- **Provider**: ASIC Connect
- **Options**:
  - Direct API (paid, requires agreement)
  - Third-party aggregators (e.g., ABR data services)
- **Data returned**:
  - Company officers (directors, secretaries)
  - Shareholders
  - Registration date
  - Company status

#### 4.3.5 Claude API (Anthropic)
- **Model**: Claude 3.5 Sonnet (balance of cost/quality)
- **Use cases**:
  - Tender summarization
  - Requirements extraction
  - Fit analysis (future)
- **Prompt template** (example):
```
Analyze this government tender and provide:
1. Plain-English summary (2-3 sentences)
2. Key requirements (bullet points)
3. Evaluation criteria
4. Complexity estimate (Low/Medium/High)
5. Key dates

Tender: {tender_content}
```

## 5. Data Models

### 5.1 Core Entities

```sql
-- Users (managed by Clerk, extended in our DB)
CREATE TABLE user_profiles (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  clerk_user_id VARCHAR(255) UNIQUE NOT NULL,

  -- Company details (from ABN lookup)
  abn VARCHAR(11),
  company_name VARCHAR(255),
  trading_name VARCHAR(255),
  entity_type VARCHAR(100),
  gst_registered BOOLEAN,
  registered_address TEXT,
  registration_date DATE,

  -- Directors (from ASIC)
  directors JSONB, -- [{name, appointed_date}]

  -- User preferences
  industry_codes TEXT[], -- UNSPSC codes
  preferred_locations TEXT[],
  min_contract_value INTEGER,
  max_contract_value INTEGER,
  email_frequency VARCHAR(20) DEFAULT 'daily',

  -- Subscription
  subscription_tier VARCHAR(50) DEFAULT 'free',
  subscription_expires_at TIMESTAMP,

  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  updated_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Tenders (aggregated from all sources)
CREATE TABLE tenders (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),

  -- Source identification
  source VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL, -- 'nsw', 'federal'
  source_id VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL, -- Original ID from source
  source_url TEXT,

  -- Core details
  title TEXT NOT NULL,
  description TEXT,
  agency_name VARCHAR(255),
  agency_id VARCHAR(100),

  -- Classification
  category_codes TEXT[], -- UNSPSC codes
  location VARCHAR(100),

  -- Financials
  estimated_value_min INTEGER,
  estimated_value_max INTEGER,

  -- Dates
  published_date TIMESTAMP,
  close_date TIMESTAMP,

  -- Status
  status VARCHAR(50), -- 'open', 'closed', 'awarded', 'cancelled'

  -- AI Summary
  ai_summary JSONB, -- {summary, requirements, criteria, complexity, key_dates}
  ai_summarized_at TIMESTAMP,

  -- Documents
  documents JSONB, -- [{name, url, type}]

  -- Indexing
  search_vector TSVECTOR,

  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  updated_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),

  UNIQUE(source, source_id)
);

-- User's tender pipeline
CREATE TABLE user_tenders (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id UUID REFERENCES user_profiles(id),
  tender_id UUID REFERENCES tenders(id),

  -- Pipeline stage
  stage VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL, -- 'discovered', 'evaluating', 'preparing', 'submitted', 'won', 'lost', 'no_bid'

  -- User notes
  notes TEXT,

  -- Tracking
  match_score DECIMAL(3,2), -- 0.00 to 1.00
  added_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  stage_changed_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),

  -- Outcome
  outcome_value INTEGER, -- Actual contract value if won

  UNIQUE(user_id, tender_id)
);

-- Award notices (for competitor tracking)
CREATE TABLE awards (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  tender_id UUID REFERENCES tenders(id),

  winner_name VARCHAR(255),
  winner_abn VARCHAR(11),
  contract_value INTEGER,
  award_date DATE,

  source VARCHAR(50),
  source_id VARCHAR(255),

  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Competitor watchlist
CREATE TABLE competitor_watchlist (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id UUID REFERENCES user_profiles(id),

  competitor_name VARCHAR(255),
  competitor_abn VARCHAR(11),
  notes TEXT,

  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),

  UNIQUE(user_id, competitor_abn)
);

-- Tender writers (marketplace)
CREATE TABLE tender_writers (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  clerk_user_id VARCHAR(255),

  -- Profile
  display_name VARCHAR(255),
  company_name VARCHAR(255),
  bio TEXT,

  -- Specializations
  industry_specializations TEXT[],
  geographic_coverage TEXT[],

  -- Pricing
  price_range VARCHAR(50), -- 'budget', 'mid', 'premium'
  hourly_rate_min INTEGER,
  hourly_rate_max INTEGER,

  -- Verification
  verified BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE,
  verified_at TIMESTAMP,

  -- Stats
  total_reviews INTEGER DEFAULT 0,
  average_rating DECIMAL(2,1),

  -- Contact
  contact_email VARCHAR(255),
  website_url TEXT,

  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW(),
  updated_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Writer reviews
CREATE TABLE writer_reviews (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  writer_id UUID REFERENCES tender_writers(id),
  reviewer_user_id UUID REFERENCES user_profiles(id),

  rating INTEGER CHECK (rating >= 1 AND rating <= 5),
  review_text TEXT,

  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Saved searches
CREATE TABLE saved_searches (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id UUID REFERENCES user_profiles(id),

  name VARCHAR(100),
  filters JSONB, -- {keywords, categories, locations, value_range, etc}

  notify_new_matches BOOLEAN DEFAULT TRUE,

  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

-- Email notification log
CREATE TABLE notification_log (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id UUID REFERENCES user_profiles(id),

  notification_type VARCHAR(50), -- 'digest', 'deadline', 'award'
  tender_ids UUID[],

  sent_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);
```

## 10. Risk Assessment

### Technical Risks

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| API rate limits | High | Medium | Implement caching, respect limits, batch requests |
| API changes/deprecation | High | Low | Abstract integrations, monitor changelogs |
| Claude API costs | Medium | Medium | Cache summaries, implement usage limits, optimize prompts |
| Data accuracy | High | Medium | Validate against multiple sources, user feedback loop |

### Business Risks

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| Low user acquisition | High | Medium | Focus on SEO, content marketing, partnerships |
| Free tier abuse | Medium | High | Implement limits, track usage patterns |
| Competition from incumbents | Medium | Medium | Focus on UX differentiation, speed to market |
| Government portal changes | High | Low | Build relationships, stay informed, adapt quickly |

### Compliance Risks

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| Data privacy (ABN/ASIC) | High | Low | Review API terms, implement data retention policies |
| Advice liability | High | Medium | Clear disclaimers, no guarantee of outcomes |
| Writer marketplace liability | Medium | Medium | Clear terms, verified writers only |

## Appendix B: Competitor Analysis Detail

### AusTender (tenders.gov.au)
- **Strengths**: Official federal source, comprehensive data, free
- **Weaknesses**: Federal only, clunky search, no alerts, raw data presentation
- **Opportunity**: Better UX, NSW integration, AI analysis

### NSW eTendering (tenders.nsw.gov.au)
- **Strengths**: Official NSW source, real-time data
- **Weaknesses**: NSW only, basic search, no application assistance
- **Opportunity**: Unified search, smart matching, auto-fill

### TenderSearch (tendersearch.com.au)
- **Strengths**: Aggregates multiple sources, established brand
- **Weaknesses**: No AI analysis, no application help, dated interface
- **Opportunity**: AI summaries, pipeline management, modern UX

### TenderBoard
- **Strengths**: Good analytics, professional focus
- **Weaknesses**: Expensive, no auto-fill, limited free tier
- **Opportunity**: Competitive pricing, auto-fill features, SME focus
